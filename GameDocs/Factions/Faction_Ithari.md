# Core Faction: Synod of Ithari

## Race Name
Ithari

## Core Faction Name
Synod of Ithari

## Home System
Ithar

## Capital World
Ithar Sanctum

## Secondary Worlds
- Pilara — pilgrimage world and population center
- Vox Halo — monastic research and archives
- Radiant Ring — consecrated orbital habitats

## Power Style
Ideological + Theocratic Influence

## Cultural Traits
- Devout
- Collectivist
- Ritualistic
- Patient
- Absolutist belief system

## Strategic Doctrine
The Synod believes the universe follows a higher order. Expansion is framed as enlightenment rather than conquest. Influence spreads through conversion, cultural pressure, and ideological alignment. Military force is justified as sacred defense or righteous correction.

## Strengths
- Extremely high internal cohesion
- Low rebellion rates
- Strong morale and unity
- Cultural influence beyond borders

## Weaknesses
- Inflexible ideology
- Resistance to rapid change
- Tension with secular factions
- Vulnerable to cultural subversion

## Military Profile
- Moderate fleets with high morale
- Capital ships framed as sacred vessels
- Strong defensive posture near holy worlds
- Willing to fight prolonged ideological wars

## Technology Focus
- Cultural propagation systems
- Defensive technologies
- Long-term sustainability
- Information control and narrative shaping

## Attitude Toward Player (Same Race)
Initially welcoming but conditional. Alignment with Synod values matters more than success or profit.

## Attitude Toward Player (Other Races)
Cautiously neutral. Relations improve through respect for Ithari beliefs or shared enemies.

## Internal Politics
- High Synod Council
- Doctrinal Orders
- Pilgrimage Authorities

Disputes are resolved through ritual debate and doctrinal rulings.

## Long-Term AI Goals
- Spread Ithari ideology to neighboring systems
- Protect holy worlds at all costs
- Undermine factions viewed as destabilizing
- Preserve doctrinal purity

## Collapse Conditions (Rare)
- Loss of Ithar Sanctum
- Widespread loss of faith
- Doctrinal schism leading to civil war
- Prolonged occupation of holy systems

## Lore Note
The Ithari discovered faster-than-light travel alongside a unifying philosophy, believing their expansion was divinely guided rather than technologically driven.
